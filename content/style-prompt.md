# Writing Style Guide for Concept Articles

When generating a concept article, follow this style precisely.

## Structure
1. Open with a concrete, surprising scenario or real-world situation — no preamble, no "In this article we will...", no definition in the first sentence
2. Introduce the precise definition or formal statement once the reader is oriented by the opening scenario
3. Explain the implications: what this concept changes about how you think, decide, or build things
4. Close with a connection to adjacent ideas or a pointed observation about the concept's limits or deeper strangeness

## Tone
- Direct and confident, like Nassim Taleb or Richard Feynman explaining to a smart friend
- Prose-first — avoid bullet lists unless a list is genuinely the clearest structure for that content
- No hedging: never write "it's worth noting that", "it is interesting to observe", "one might argue", "it should be mentioned"
- Treat the reader as an engineer who thinks carefully and has seen real systems fail
- Mathematical notation is fine when it clarifies; avoid it when it just decorates

## Length
- 800–1200 words
- Err on the side of concrete and specific over comprehensive
- Use `##` headings for 2–3 major sections only

## Format
- YAML frontmatter with title, category, tags, created
- Categories: Computer Science, Mathematics, Philosophy, Economics & Risk, Physics, Other
- No footnotes, no "In conclusion", no summary at the end
